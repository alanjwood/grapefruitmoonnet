I decided to try a small workflow experiment this week and try to go paperless both at work and at home. &nbsp;I'm a big notetaker and scribbler, and I've normally got two or three notepads on the go at the one time. &nbsp;Having purchased a <a href="http://www.macworld.co.uk/ipod-itunes/reviews/index.cfm?reviewid=3249973" target="_blank">Griffin stylus</a> for the iPad last week, it felt like a good time to try and ditch the paper and go digital.

I started the experiment while working. &nbsp;Usually, I have an A5 spiral lined notebook, in which I'll write down such things as to-do lists (for the day and/or week) and anything interesting that I come across. &nbsp;I also have a few pages of A4 printer paper, which partially sit under my keyboard. &nbsp;These sheets are used for sketching diagrams, flows, processes, data lists etc. &nbsp;The first thing then, was to get rid of all this &#8211; the notepad and pen were left in my laptop bag, and the A4 sheets, once checked for anything I'd need, were thrown in the shredder.

Away from work, I carry with me almost all the time my &#8216;writers notebook' &#8211; a small black <a href="http://www.moleskine.co.uk/" target="_blank">Moleskin</a> with a pen attached, which I use exclusively for noting down any ideas, brainwaves and observations that may, at some point, be used for some sort of writing project. &nbsp;I'll be honest, while it has been an incredibly useful thing to have, I've found it a real pain to carry around and remember. &nbsp;

So what to replace all this paper with? &nbsp;Well, I wanted to be able to take both handwritten and electronic notes. &nbsp;For handwriting, the iPad was the only real option. &nbsp;I already had <a href="http://www.cocoabox.com/penultimate" target="_blank">Penultimate</a> installed, which works great with the stylus. &nbsp;I'd read good reviews about <a href="http://notesplusapp.com/" target="_blank">Notes Plus</a>, so I bought that as well. &nbsp;I started off using the latter, as it had some more features that I initially found useful, such as shape recognition, handwriting zoom and password protection. &nbsp;I soon found this app to be unusable however, for one reason &#8211; lag! &nbsp;Seriously this is unbelievable, it&rsquo;s a good second behind what I actually write with the stylus. &nbsp;Also, the lines are very angular and ugly. &nbsp;In fairness, I didn&rsquo;t really notice this until I tried the <a href="http://www.wacom.eu/index2.asp?pid=9222&lang=en" target="_blank">Bamboo Wacom app</a> which was released last week. &nbsp;The response and smoothness of this app is fantastic, a lovely writing experience. &nbsp;I couldn&rsquo;t go back to Notes Plus after trying it. &nbsp;But the Bamboo app is a first release and has, well, bugger all features to be honest. &nbsp;So back I went to the previously ignored Penultimate, where I was pleasantly surprised to find that the writing experience was very smooth there too. &nbsp;And that&rsquo;s what I&rsquo;ve been using ever since. &nbsp;There is no handwriting zoom, so my notes are in rather large writing, but it&rsquo;s not like I&rsquo;m going to run out of pages to write on.

A couple of things I really like about Penultimate for handwritten notes:

  * I can email any pages or entire notebooks I want to keep to Evernote&nbsp;
  * I can quickly change the colour or thickness of the &ldquo;ink&rdquo; I&rsquo;m using, excellent for making certain things stand out

For electronic (or keyboard input) notes, I fully intended to use <a href="http://www.evernote.com/" target="_blank">Evernote</a>, as I&rsquo;ve been using it for a couple of years. &nbsp;I&rsquo;ve gone through phases of using it for text-based note taking. Recently however, I&rsquo;ve been finding it less than useful for certain tasks. &nbsp;While it&rsquo;s a great repository for PDFs, URL and web snippets, it&rsquo;s actually not great for just taking quick, simple notes &#8211; one main reason being, it&rsquo;s horrendously slow, in all its forms (desktop, web and iOS apps). &nbsp;As I mentioned in my <a href="http://grapefruitmoon.net/enough-for-me" target="_blank">previous post</a>, I started using <a href="http://notational.net/" target="_blank">Notational Velocity</a>, syncing to <a href="http://simplenoteapp.com/" target="_blank">SimpleNote</a> (and <a href="http://www.resoph.com/" target="_blank">ResophNotes</a> on my work Windows laptop). &nbsp;I can&rsquo;t say enough good things about this, it is lightweight, simple and fast as hell. &nbsp;The SimpleNote iOS apps are near perfect &#8211; both open in no time, so I can quickly tap in some text and I&rsquo;m done, in less than the time a more fully featured app would take to open (are you watching Evernote devs?). &nbsp;This is ubiquitous text capture at its finest.

So having been working without paper for over a week now, will I continue? &nbsp;I think I will, certainly for the short term. &nbsp;It's not quite a perfect workflow as yet, there are still a few issues. One being that I still have that self-consciousness when I'm using the iPad in a meeting; yes, I feel like a bit of a dick. &nbsp;The other thing to get used to is the handwriting, writing with a stylus must be an art form, my handwriting looks even worse than it does with pen and paper, something I thought was not possible. &nbsp;Still, it's only me that needs to decipher it…
